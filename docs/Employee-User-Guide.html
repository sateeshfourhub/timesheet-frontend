Getting Started — Create Your Account

One-time setup · 2 minutes

If your administrator has not already created an account for you, you can register yourself. If an account was created for you, skip to Section 2 — Logging In.


  1. Open your browser and go to https://timesheet.fourhubtech.com
  2. On the login page, click the "Create an account" or "Sign Up" link at the bottom of the form.
  3. Fill in the registration form:

    Field What to enter
    Full Name Your first and last name (e.g. John Smith)
    Email Address Your work email address — this is your login username
    Company Name Your organisation / company name
    Password Choose a secure password — at least 8 characters
  4. Click "Register" or "Create Account". You will be automatically logged in and taken to your weekly timesheet.

Understanding the Weekly View

Your main screen

After logging in, you will see the Weekly Timesheet — your main working screen. Here is what everything means:


Screenshot: Weekly Timesheet view — full screen

Add a screenshot of the main weekly view with day dials here

‹ Prev / Next ›
Navigate between weeks. You can view the current week and the previous week. Future weeks are locked unless your admin enables future access for you.
Week Label
Shows the date range for the week you are currently viewing — e.g. "Apr 28 – May 4, 2026".
Day Dials (circles)
One circle for each day of the week (Mon–Sun). A coloured dial means hours have been logged for that day. Click any day to add or edit hours.
Days Logged
Shows how many days have complete time entries (clock-in AND clock-out) for the current week.
Total Logged
The total hours from clock-in to clock-out across all days (includes break time).
Break Time
Total break minutes deducted across all days this week.
Net Worked
Your actual working hours after break time is subtracted. This is what gets recorded when you submit.
Submit Week →
Finalises your timesheet for the week and notifies your manager. You can only submit once per week.

Status indicators on the day dials:

Green filled dial — Hours logged for that day with clock-out recorded
Empty / grey dial — No hours logged yet for that day
Blue outlined dial — Today

Logging Your Hours

Do this every working day

To log hours for a day, click on the day dial (the circle) for that day. A popup window will appear where you enter your times.


  1. On the weekly view, click the circle (dial) for the day you want to log. For example, click the "Mon" circle to log Monday's hours.
  2. The Time Entry popup will open. Fill in the following fields:

    Field What to enter Example
    Clock In The time you started work 09:00 AM
    Clock Out The time you finished work 05:30 PM
    Break (minutes) Total break time in minutes (optional) 30
    Notes Any additional notes (optional) Client meeting in the morning
  3. The popup will automatically calculate your Net Hours (Clock Out minus Clock In minus Break time). Check this looks correct.
  4. Click "Save". The day dial will turn green and show your clock-in and clock-out times.

Editing or Deleting an Entry

Fix a mistake

Made a mistake? You can update or remove any entry as long as the week has not yet been submitted.


To edit an entry:

  1. Click the day dial for the day you want to change. The Time Entry popup will open showing the existing times already filled in.
  2. Update the Clock In, Clock Out, Break or Notes fields as needed.
  3. Click "Save" to apply your changes.

To delete an entry:

  1. Click the day dial for the day you want to clear.
  2. In the popup, click the "Delete" button (usually shown in red at the bottom of the popup).
  3. The entry will be removed and the day dial will return to empty.

Submitting Your Week

Do this at the end of every work week

When you have finished logging all your hours for the week, you need to submit your timesheet. Submitting sends your hours to your manager and records them permanently.


  1. Make sure you have logged hours for all working days of the week, with a Clock Out time for each day.
  2. Check the summary at the bottom of the screen — Total Logged, Break Time, and Net Worked. Make sure these look correct.
  3. Click the "Submit Week →" button on the right side of the screen.
  4. A green confirmation message will appear at the top of the screen confirming how many days and hours were submitted. Your manager will also receive a notification email.
  5. The "Submit Week →" button will change to a "✓ Submitted" badge in green. This confirms your timesheet is locked in for the week.

Locked Weeks & Future Access

What you can and cannot edit

The application controls which weeks you are allowed to edit. This prevents errors and ensures timesheets are submitted on time.


Week Your Access What you can do
This week ✓ Always open Add, edit, delete entries and submit
Last week ✓ Always open Add, edit, delete entries and submit
2+ weeks ago 🔒 Locked View only — contact admin to edit
Future weeks ⚙️ By permission Only if your admin has granted Future Access — up to 4 weeks ahead maximum

What does "Locked — contact admin to edit" mean?


If you see the amber message "Locked — contact admin to edit" below the week dates, it means you cannot make changes to that week. This happens when:

  • The week is more than one week in the past
  • The week is in the future and you have not been granted future access
  • The week is more than 4 weeks into the future (even with future access)

Frequently Asked Questions

I can't log in — what should I do?
Check that you are using the correct email address and password. If you have forgotten your password, contact your administrator — they can reset it from the Admin Dashboard.
The "Submit Week" button is greyed out — why?
The submit button is disabled if you have no completed entries for the week (entries must have both a Clock In and Clock Out time). Log at least one full day first, then the button will become active.
I submitted the wrong hours — can I undo it?
Submissions cannot be undone by employees. Contact your administrator immediately. They can assist in correcting the record from the Admin Dashboard.
I didn't receive a confirmation email after submitting.
Check your spam/junk folder first. The email comes from the Fourhub Timesheet system. If it still hasn't arrived after 10 minutes, contact your administrator — the "✓ Submitted" badge on screen confirms your submission regardless of the email.
Can I log hours for weekends?
Yes. Saturday and Sunday appear in the weekly view and work exactly the same as weekdays. Click the weekend day dial to add hours.
I need to log hours for a week more than one week ago.
By default, you can only edit the current week and the previous week. For weeks older than that, contact your administrator — they have access to make corrections.
What does "Net Worked" mean?
Net Worked = Clock Out time minus Clock In time, minus your break time. For example: 9:00 AM to 5:30 PM with a 30-minute break = 8 hours net worked. This is the figure that gets recorded when you submit.
I see a message "You have already submitted your timesheet for this week."
This means you already submitted this week's timesheet. The "✓ Submitted" green badge will appear instead of the Submit button. If you believe this is an error, contact your administrator.
Who do I contact for help?
Contact your administrator or the Fourhub Tech team at sateesh@fourhubtech.com for any issues with the application.
